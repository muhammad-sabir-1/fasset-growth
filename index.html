Growth Engagement.

We build an experiment-based growth system for Fasset. This document outlines three initial focus areas, funnel, Meta creative, and Reddit, and how we work with your team to run them.

01

Activation experiments

This is likely the biggest lever, since it directly affects CAC, spend per user, and more. A simple process for the first experiments:

  1. Audit your app, screen by screen, with real drop-off numbers. Install to sign-up to basic KYC to full KYC to first deposit to first action to day 7/30. The biggest drop becomes the first test.
  2. Tear down the competition the same way, regional and global. Where they have publicly A/B tested their onboarding, we borrow the proven winner.
  3. Turn gaps into flag-gated experiments your engineers ship without a meeting. We spec, they flag, we read, they keep or revert.

The teardown

The artifact we produce is a screen-by-screen teardown, with the drop-off and the job each screen is doing. Examples of the format:

We map the flow install → sign-up → basic KYC → full KYC → first deposit → first action → day 7/30, and for each screen record step conversion, drop-off %, time on step, and the one job that screen should do. Public reviews suggest the deep leak is post-KYC (stuck withdrawals, freezes), which the audit confirms or kills with data.

Tools: analytics (PostHog default, or Amplitude / Mixpanel), and feature flags (PostHog built-in).

The backlog

04

How we work

Define success

Start with a clear definition of success, so every experiment ladders up to one number. For Fasset that might be total active users holding at least $10 in their wallet. Acquisition metrics like CAC are then measured against that, rather than against installs.

From our initial discussions, the experiments focus on two areas. Acquisition is experimentation around creative, angles, and channels. Activation is funnel work to lift conversion from signup, to KYC-complete, to first spend — does that click turn into a funded user?

We set each test's target before it runs, then read it against that target. For a new creative, measured on ROAS at a set spend:

Green — at or above the ROAS target on meaningful spend. Scale.
Yellow — within reach of the target, not there yet. Watch.
Red — still short of target after meaningful spend. Kill.

Targets hold for the length of the test. No moving goalposts. Once this framework is in place, the same approach carries across every experiment, and growth becomes systemized.

Run growth like engineering

We run growth the way an engineering team runs a roadmap. Every experiment and task lives in Linear, so the whole thing stays visible and tracked. A monthly planning sprint sets the batch of experiments, and a weekly review reads results and adjusts. We write the tickets — hypothesis, change, metric, kill point — and your team executes them.

Each month runs the same loop: refresh the numbers, design the next batch, hand them over specced and ready, then read out what won, what got killed, and what is next.

We do the thinking and the measurement. Your team does the posting and shipping. Small improvements compound month over month.
